# Adding sources

Miyura doesn't host content itself. Instead, you add **sources** — endpoints that the reader fetches series and chapters from.

## A note before you continue

Miyura is a tool. The sources you choose to connect are your decision and your responsibility. The project does not endorse any specific source, and we ask that you respect the rights of creators and prioritize official, licensed releases where they exist.

## Adding a source

1. Open Miyura.
2. Go to **Browse → Sources**.
3. Tap **Add source**.
4. Enter the source URL or pick from a list of sources you've previously added.

Once added, sources appear in the Browse tab. You can search across them, filter by genre or language, and add anything you find to your library.

## Managing sources

- **Reorder** sources by drag and drop in the source list.
- **Disable** a source temporarily without removing it.
- **Remove** a source entirely from the source settings.

## Source health

Sources occasionally break — websites change their layouts, certificates expire, that sort of thing. Miyura will surface a warning when a source isn't responding, and the community keeps a list of known-good sources in the project repository.
