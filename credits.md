# Credits

Miyura is built and maintained by volunteers. There is no company behind it, no funding round, no full-time staff — just people who like the idea of a clean, open reader and donate their time.

## How contributors are credited

We do this in three places, and we keep them up to date:

1. **The repository** — every commit has its author. The full contributor graph is on GitHub.
2. **`CONTRIBUTORS.md`** — a maintained file in the repo listing significant contributors, organized by area (code, design, docs, translations).
3. **In-app About screen** — a rotating credits list shown in the app's About page.

## Areas of contribution

People help in lots of ways:

- **Code.** Features, bug fixes, refactors, performance work.
- **Design.** UI, UX, illustration, brand.
- **Translation.** Miyura is translated by community volunteers via the project's localization workflow.
- **Documentation.** This site, the in-app help, the wiki.
- **Community.** Issue triage, helping new users, code review.
- **Testing.** Filing thoughtful bug reports, reproducing edge cases, testing release candidates.

All of these count. We try not to privilege code over the rest.

## Project leadership

Miyura is maintained by a small group of long-term contributors who hold commit and release rights. Decisions about direction, breaking changes, and code of conduct enforcement rest with this group, and are made in the open whenever possible.

The current maintainer list is in [`MAINTAINERS.md`](https://github.com/miyura/miyura/blob/main/MAINTAINERS.md) in the repository.

## Thank you

If you've ever filed a bug, written a translation string, reviewed a PR, or just told someone about the project — thank you. The site, the app, and the community wouldn't exist without you.

If you'd like to start contributing, the [Contributing guide](/contributing/guide) is a good place to begin.
