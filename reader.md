# Reader basics

The reader is the heart of Miyura. It's deliberately simple — most things are tappable or swipeable, and the controls fade out so the page is what you see.

## Reading modes

- **Paged (LTR)** — left-to-right paging. The default for Western comics and webtoons read top-down.
- **Paged (RTL)** — right-to-left paging. The default for Japanese manga.
- **Continuous vertical** — long strip mode, ideal for webtoons.
- **Continuous horizontal** — for double-page spreads.

You can set a default mode globally and override it per series.

## Controls

- **Tap left / right** of the screen to page back / forward.
- **Tap the center** to toggle the reader UI (chapter info, progress bar, settings).
- **Pinch** to zoom in continuous mode.
- **Long press** on a page to save or share it (where the source allows).

## Per-series settings

Each series remembers its own reading mode, page layout, and brightness. Changes you make in the reader for one series don't affect anything else.

## Offline reading

When you're online and on a good connection, you can pre-download chapters by tapping **Download** on a series page. Downloaded chapters are stored on the device and read with no network needed.

::: warning Storage matters
Manga and comics can take up real storage. Miyura shows you how much space the offline cache is using, and you can clear it any time from Settings → Storage.
:::

## Continue reading

Miyura opens to the chapter you were on, at the page you left. There's nothing to configure — it's just how the app works.
